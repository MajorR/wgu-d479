TRAVEL & TRANSPORTATION

Travel & Transportation

How do I get to Taniti Island?

Taniti is accessible by air through our small airport with daily flights from major Pacific airports. You can also arrive by sea via private boat, small cruise ship, or ferry services from nearby islands.

Do I need a visa to visit Taniti?

Most visitors can stay on Taniti for up to 30 days without a visa. However, requirements vary by country of origin. Please check with your local Taniti consulate or embassy for specific requirements.

ACCOMMODATIONS

Accommodations

What types of accommodations are available?

Taniti offers a range of accommodations including luxury resorts, boutique hotels, beach bungalows, and eco-lodges. Options are available for various budgets and preferences.

Is Wi-Fi available at all accommodations?

Most accommodations offer Wi-Fi, though connection speeds may vary. Luxury resorts and hotels typically have the most reliable connections, while some remote eco-lodges may have limited or no connectivity.

Are there family-friendly accommodations?

Yes, many resorts and hotels cater to families with amenities such as kids' clubs, family rooms, and child-friendly activities. Please specify your needs when booking.

How far in advance should I book my accommodation?

We recommend booking at least 3-6 months in advance, especially during the high season (June to September). For holiday periods, booking 6-12 months ahead is advisable.

ACTIVITIES & EXPERIENCES

Activities & Experiences

What are the most popular activities on Taniti?

The most popular activities include snorkeling and diving at the coral reefs, hiking to the volcano, exploring the rainforest, and experiencing local cultural events and cuisine.

Is it safe to visit the volcano?

Yes, when accompanied by authorized guides. Our volcano tours follow strict safety protocols, and the activity level is constantly monitored. Tours may be canceled if conditions are deemed unsafe.

Are there activities suitable for children?

Absolutely! We offer family-friendly beach activities, gentle rainforest walks, cultural workshops, and wildlife viewing opportunities that are perfect for children of all ages.

Do I need to book activities in advance?

For popular activities like volcano tours and cultural performances, we recommend booking in advance. Other activities can often be arranged upon arrival, but booking ahead ensures availability.

PRACTICAL INFORMATION

Practical Information

What is the currency used on Taniti?

The local currency is the Taniti Dollar (TND), but US dollars are widely accepted. Major credit cards are accepted at most hotels, restaurants, and larger shops.

What is the best time of year to visit?

Taniti enjoys a tropical climate year-round. The dry season (June to September) is the most popular time to visit. The rainy season (November to March) offers fewer crowds but occasional heavy showers.

Are there any cultural customs I should be aware of?

Taniti has a respectful culture. When visiting villages or religious sites, modest dress is appreciated. Always ask permission before taking photos of locals, and remove shoes when entering someone's home.

Is the tap water safe to drink?

In major hotels and resorts, tap water is generally safe. However, we recommend drinking bottled water, which is readily available throughout the island, especially in more remote areas.
